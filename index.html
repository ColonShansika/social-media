Shirley Chisholm

Shirley Anita Chisholm was an American politician who, in 1968, became the first black woman to be elected to the United States Congress.

  • Born: November 30, 1924
  • Notable for: She was the first Black woman elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1968.
  1. She was the first African American woman elected to Congress.
  2. She was the first woman to run for the Democratic Party's presidential nomination.

Alice Coachman

Alice Marie Coachman Davis was an American athlete. She specialized in high jump and was the first black woman to win an Olympic gold medal.

  • Born: November 9, 1923
  • Notable for: The first Black woman from any country to win an Olympic gold medal
  1. Won 25 national titles, most of them in the high jump
  2. Coachman also created the Alice Coachman Track and Field Foundation to help young athletes.
